Home Lab

I started assembling my Home Lab in early 2023 starting with a $100 mini PC from Amazon and a switch my friend sold me for $10
Since then, I have added quite a few components, including a 24U rack that I somehow managed to fit in my room.
I currently run a NAS on the mini PC, and PiHole on my Raspberry Pi, plus many other various projects on my bigger, more powerful machines.

Specifications:

  • Rack: Dell 2410 24U
  • Monitor: Acer X203H 20" 1600x900
  • Router: Mikrotik RB3011UIAS-RM 10xGigabit Ethernet
  • Switch: ZyXEL GS1910-24 24-Port GbE Managed Switch 4x SFP Ports
  • Hyve Zeus V1 1U Server
  • Raspberry Pi 4 w/ Argon ONE V2 Case
  • Beelink Mini PC
  • "Frankenstein" Desktop/Server w/ Cooler Master HAF XB EVO Case

Custom PC Build

I've officially been building computers for over a decade, with my first being a very budget gaming computer I built with my dad back in 2014.
This computer was fast for the time, but didn't look very cool. It was thrown into a ancient case that my dad had laying around that was practically falling apart.
Since then, I have upgraded all of my components (some more than once) and now have a great looking and speedy computer that I use for gaming, homework and other projects.
Most of the old components that I have upgraded over the years have been thrown into my "Frankenstein" machine that lives in my server rack, which I use for all kinds of cool projects.

Specifications:

  • Case: Corsair Carbide Series SPEC-DELTA RGB
  • CPU: AMD Ryzen™ 5 5600x
  • CPU Cooler: Termalright Phantom Spirit 120 EVO
  • GPU: ZOTAC GAMING GeForce RTX 3070 Twin Edge
  • Motherboard: MSI MAG B550 Tomahawk
  • RAM: Corsair VENGEANCE® DDR4 DRAM 2x8GB / VENGEANCE® RGB Pro DDR4 DRAM 2x8GB @ 3200MHz
  • Storage: Samsung 870 EVO SATA SSD / Western Digital 2TB WD Blue SATA SSD
  • PSU: Corsair CX Series™ CX750F RGB - 750 Watt PSU
  • Case Fans: Corsair LL120 RGB 120mm Dual List RGB PWM Fan

Custom Keyboard Build

I never thought I would be the kind of person to spend a ridiculous amount of money on a keyboard, but here we are.
I also never thought I would be the kind of person to own a keyboard without a numpad, but even I surprise myself sometimes.
The main reason I built this keyboard was just as a cool project and so that I could say that I did.
It also has a cool volume knob.

Specifications:

  • Frame: GK GAMAKAY LK67 RGB Hotswappable Mechanical Keyboard
  • Switches: Kailh Box Jade Mechanical 3 Pin Keyboard Switches
  • Caps: Gliging Blue Gradient Keycaps
